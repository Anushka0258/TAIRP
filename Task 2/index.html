Misal Pav

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup wheat flour
  • ▢1 cup coarse wheat flour
  • ▢Salt to taste
  • ▢1 tbsp cumin seeds
  • ▢2 tbsp sesame seeds
  • ▢½ tsp asafetida - hing
  • ▢½ tsp ajwain
  • ▢1 tsp turmeric powder
  • ▢1 tbsp red chilli powder
  • ▢1 tbsp coriander powder
  • ▢½ tsp garam masala
  • ▢¼ cup coriander leaves
  • ▢6 tbsp oil
  • ▢¾ cup water or as required
  • ▢Ghee for roasting

    Instructions:

    1. In a mixing bowl, combine wheat flour, coarse wheat flour, and salt.
    2. Crush cumin seeds, white sesame seeds, and ajwain using a rolling pin and add them to the flour mixture.
    3. Add hing, turmeric powder, red chilli powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and chopped coriander leaves.
    4. Pour in the oil and mix everything well with your hands. The flour should have a crumbly texture and bind together when pressed between your hands.
    5. Gradually add water while kneading to form a tight dough for the bhakri.
    6. Cover the dough and let it rest for 10-15 minutes.
    7. Take a medium-sized portion of the dough and roll it out into a bigger-sized, medium-thick bhakri.
    8. Use a small-sized lid to cut out bhakri pieces and repeat the process with the remaining dough.
    9. Heat a tawa or griddle on medium heat and place the rolled bhakri on it.
    10. Cook each portion of the bhakri dough on both sides until golden brown, applying light pressure using a wooden press to ensure even cooking.
    11. Cook the bhakhri on medium heat until golden brown spots appear on both sides.
    12. Apply some ghee on both sides of the bhakhri and roast it for an additional 2 minutes to make it flakier.
    13. Serve the biscuit bhakhri immediately or store it in an airtight container.

Pav Bhaji

Ingredients:

  • 4 potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, bell peppers), boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add chopped onions. Sauté until they turn golden brown.
  2. Add minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  4. Add mashed potatoes, mashed vegetables, pav bhaji masala, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, adding water as needed. Mash everything together.
  6. Serve hot with buttered pav (buns) and chopped onions.
